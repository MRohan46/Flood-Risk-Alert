# Project Story

## Inspiration
The inspiration for **Flood Alert** was the observation of the heartbreaking consequences of floods in our society, especially in rural and low-lying parts of Pakistan. numerous families lose assets and even lives because of delayed notifications and the absence of timely information. We aimed to develop a mechanism that would offer **early warnings based on real-time data**, ensuring people remain safe and alert.

Our motivation was a combination of **concern for the environment, community work, and technology**𨫎e wanted to create something that actually could make an impact.

## What it does
**Flood Alert** is a smart alert system which **forecasts likely flood hazards and warns users beforehand**. It aggregates data from a variety of sources, including:

- **Rainwater and water level sensors**
- **Weather APIs for live forecasting**
- **Geographical and historical flood data**

Based on this data, the system estimates the risk and sends **SMS, mobile alerts, or email alerts**. It also offers practical advice, such as evacuation paths and safe areas.

Mathematically, the prediction of risk is a weighted sum of parameters:

\[
Flood Risk Score = w_1 \cdot Rainfall + w_2 \cdot River Level + w_3 \cdot Soil Saturation + w_4 \cdot Historical Flood Data
] 

where \( w_1, w_2, w_3, w_4 \) are weights that were found through experimentation and calibration.

## How we built it
We developed **Flood Alert** with a **MERN stack** for the web application: 

- **MongoDB** for storing user data, historical records, and sensor logs
- **Node.js** and **Express.js** for the backend API and alerting logic
- **React.js** for the user interface and notifications dashboard

To implement real-time alerts, we used **Twilio API for SMS** and **Firebase Cloud Messaging** for push notifications. Processing of data and flood risk computation were done using **Python scripts** that fetch data from APIs and IoT sensors and send the outputs to the backend.

The design provides **scalability and quick response**, even under concurrent user connections.

## Issues we encountered
- **Reliability of data:** Weather and sensor readings sometimes were inconsistent or lagged, necessitating the use of smoothing algorithms and backup data sources.
- **Merging multiple APIs:** Merging various data sources into one predictive model was problematic, particularly when units or formats weren't compatible.
- **Time limitations:** As a hackathon project, we only had time to **develop, test, and polish** the system.
- **Alert precision:** Calibrating the notification thresholds to prevent false alerts without compromising safety was difficult.

## Things that we're proud of
- Developed a **working prototype** that was able to send flood alerts in real-time.
- Were able to **merge various data sources** and integrate a predictive model.
- Designed a **useful, clean dashboard** that shows alerts, risk scores, and safe zones.
- Built a solution that has **practical use potential**, particularly in areas that are prone to floods.

## What we learned
